## 1. Study Design
**Recommendation:** Two-stage prognostic validation. **Stage A:** multicenter *retrospective* longitudinal cohort with a **frozen model** (weights, thresholds, and fusion locked before any outcome is unblinded) to estimate discrimination and calibration for 12-month relapse. **Stage B:** *prospective, geographically external* validation at IBD centers not contributing training data, enrolling adults in remission/mild activity at an index ileocolonoscopy and following them 12 months to a protocol-defined flare endpoint. Report to **TRIPOD-AI**; pre-register the analysis plan. Because the output is a time-anchored prognosis, the design is a cohort with a fixed prediction horizon, not a diagnostic case-control (case-control inflates apparent performance for prognostic models).
**Rationale:** Retrieved multimodal "combined-modality prediction" precedent is a single-center completed study of only n=50 predicting postoperative deficits (NCT06712069) — useful as a design analog (combined modalities → predict a future clinical event) but far too small and off-indication to serve as evidence. No IBD relapse-prediction trial was retrieved. Literature analogs for multimodal integration are consensus imaging documents in other organ systems (e.g. PMID 34729586, PMID 28510718), which establish the multimodal-integration paradigm but not an IBD prognostic design. The retrospective-then-prospective/external structure is standard for prognostic SaMD where a locked model must prove it generalizes off its development sites.
**Confidence:** LOW — no on-point IBD prognostic trial retrieved; design is reasoned from general prognostic-model methodology.

## 2. Sensor / Input Validation (Pre-Condition)
**Recommendation:** Before any relapse-probability claim is trusted, establish measurement validity of each input stream independently: (a) **Endoscopic video** — characterize performance across endoscope vendor/model, sensor resolution, frame rate, illumination (white-light vs. virtual chromoendoscopy), and crucially **bowel-preparation quality** and **withdrawal/insertion motion blur**; require a per-study image-quality gate that rejects or flags uninterpretable frames before the ResNet branch scores them. (b) **Laboratory markers** — fecal calprotectin especially is **assay-platform dependent** (ELISA vs. lateral-flow vs. turbidimetric give non-interchangeable values); record assay and units, and validate that the tabular branch is robust to platform differences or restrict to a harmonized assay. (c) **Free text** — validate the text branch against site-specific documentation style and templating. Segment-level index validity is capped by frame-selection and pooling behavior on incomplete/aborted colonoscopies.
**Rationale:** The relevant device class is the **Endoscopic Video Imaging System/Component (product code OCS, regulation 876.1500, Class 2)** and related gastroenterology endoscopic devices (FED, OCS) — the model consumes their output, so its scores inherit those devices' acquisition variability. MAUDE malfunction reports for endoscopic accessories (report_number 1037905-2020-00191, -00201) and Class II software-related recalls (Olympus UES-40 software upgrade Z-1277-2013; Boston Scientific Auriga power-degradation Z-1396-2019) illustrate that endoscopy hardware/software failure modes are real and can silently degrade input quality. None of these validate *this* model; they establish that input-acquisition variability is a first-order threat, not a footnote.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — device class and failure-mode precedents are directly retrieved; their quantitative impact on this model is not.

## 4. Ground Truth Strategy
**Recommendation:** Two reference standards. **(1) Outcome label (relapse within 12 months):** a composite, protocol-defined endpoint — e.g. corticosteroid course, treatment escalation/change, IBD-related hospitalization or surgery, or objectively confirmed disease activity — adjudicated by a **blinded clinical events committee** to reduce label noise. Pre-specify how "clinical remission/mild activity" at baseline is defined (clinical index + objective marker). **(2) Endoscopic-severity reference** for the auxiliary head: a validated instrument (e.g. SES-CD) read by **≥2 independent central readers blinded to outcome and to model output**, with inter-reader agreement reported and disagreements adjudicated. Report the ceiling: if central-reader agreement is only moderate, achievable model agreement is capped at that level and no training can exceed it.
**Rationale:** Retrieved IBD-domain literature is mechanistic (intestinal fibrosis reversibility, PMID 28402994) rather than label-standard-defining, and no IBD endoscopic-scoring reference protocol was retrieved — so the specific instrument choice must be set by GI experts. The label-reliability-as-ceiling constraint is methodological, not citation-dependent. Blinding of readers to both outcome and model output prevents circularity.
**Confidence:** LOW — no retrieved record fixes the IBD reference standard; strategy is principled but expert-dependent.

## 5. Sample Size

Given a multimodal model with many effective degrees of freedom, the effective-parameter count for the fusion head — not just final layer size — drives requirements. **The specific N must be computed by the study team once the expected 12-month flare rate in this remission/mild population and the target precision are fixed; no defensible number can be asserted here.** Enforce a minimum event count per pre-specified subgroup (Field 6) or explicitly declare those subgroups underpowered.

## 7. Regulatory Pathway

## What This Validation Certifies — and What It Does Not
**CERTIFIES:** That, if the pre-specified study passes, the locked multimodal model stratifies 12-month Crohn's-disease relapse risk (and reproduces the human endoscopic-severity reference) with the pre-declared discrimination, calibration, and net-benefit targets, in adults in clinical remission or mild activity, at the specific IBD centers, on the specific endoscope stacks and calprotectin assay platforms, and within the pre-specified subgroups tested — as an adjunct to gastroenterologist assessment.
**DOES NOT CERTIFY:**
- That acting on the model's risk score **improves patient outcomes** (that requires an interventional/treat-to-target trial, not a prognostic cohort).
- **Physiological ground truth** — the endpoint is a protocol-defined composite and a reader-based endoscopic score, both label-noise-limited; the model cannot exceed that reference's reliability.
- **Generalization** to pediatric patients, ulcerative colitis, moderate-to-severe active disease, other endoscope vendors, or calprotectin assays not tested.
- **Distinction between active inflammation and fibrostenotic stricture**, which the image branch may conflate (PMID 28402994).
- **Regulatory clearance** — validation evidence is necessary but not sufficient; FDA (and any non-US) authorization is a separate determination.
- **Robustness to input drift** after any endoscope, software, or assay change post-deployment.
