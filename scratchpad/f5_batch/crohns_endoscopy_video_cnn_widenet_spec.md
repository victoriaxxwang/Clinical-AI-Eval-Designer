## 1. Study Design
**Recommendation:** Two linked studies. (A) **Prognostic primary study:** prospective, multi-center observational cohort enrolling adults with established Crohn's in remission/mild activity at index ileocolonoscopy, with locked model inputs captured at baseline and blinded structured follow-up to ≥12 months for the pre-defined relapse endpoint. The model output must be masked from treating clinicians during follow-up to avoid intervention bias (or treated as an explicit confounder). (B) **Endoscopic-severity sub-study:** a reader-comparison design against central adjudicated scoring, which may use retrospectively collected video. Enrollment should be consecutive/enriched-but-documented across ≥3 IBD centers to capture scope-vendor and practice heterogeneity.
**Rationale:** No retrieved trial evaluates a multimodal relapse-prediction model in Crohn's. The closest registered studies are observational IBD-management cohorts (NCT07167186, n=240, observational, endpoint = change in management/skills) and a small dietary RCT using fecal calprotectin as the inflammation readout (NCT06698601, n=60) — neither validates an AI predictor, but both confirm calprotectin-anchored inflammation endpoints and observational cohort feasibility in this population. The systematic review of deep-learning endoscopic severity assessment (PMID 42238140, Cureus 2026) confirms reader-comparison designs are the established paradigm for the severity-index component. A prospective time-to-event design is required because the primary output is a 12-month forecast, which cannot be validated on cross-sectional or retrospective-only data without immortal-time and leakage risks.

## 2. Sensor / Input Validation (Pre-Condition)
**Recommendation:** Validate each of the three input streams *before* any fusion-model claim is trusted. (a) **Endoscopic video:** pre-specify minimum acquisition standards — bowel-prep adequacy (e.g., Boston Bowel Prep or equivalent), frame rate, resolution, scope/imaging-system model, and exclusion of uninterpretable frames; quantify model degradation vs prep quality and vendor. (b) **Laboratory markers:** document assay platform, LoD/LoQ, and reference ranges for fecal calprotectin and CRP (calprotectin especially is highly assay- and stool-sampling-dependent); pre-register the assay(s) the model was trained/validated against. (c) **Free-text NLP:** validate against site-specific documentation style, templating, and negation handling before trusting the text embedding. Establish input-quality gating so the model refuses/flags out-of-distribution inputs.
**Rationale:** The pipeline consumes three heterogeneous inputs, each with distinct measurement-validity threats. MAUDE and recall signals for endoscopic hardware in the retrieved set — endoscopic guidewire malfunctions (report 1037905-2020-00191/-00201), the Olympus UES-40 software-upgrade recall (Z-1277-2013), and a laser-console power-degradation recall (Z-1396-2019, Boston Scientific) — illustrate that imaging/endoscopy hardware performance and software versions drift and fail, which directly perturbs the video branch. Bowel-ultrasound accuracy work (PMID 40817032) and PET/MR diagnostic-performance work (PMID 29531862) underscore that inflammation-imaging accuracy is modality- and site-dependent. Calprotectin variability is well documented as pre-analytic; this must be pinned down. Fecal calprotectin as a formal inflammation endpoint is validated in NCT06698601.

## 4. Ground Truth Strategy
**Recommendation:** Use two distinct reference standards. (a) **Severity index:** central, blinded, multi-reader adjudication using a validated endoscopic score (e.g., SES-CD), with inter-reader agreement reported; the model's severity output is capped by reader agreement. (b) **Relapse outcome:** a pre-registered, objective composite definition of 12-month clinically significant relapse (e.g., a combination of symptom scores, biomarker elevation, treatment escalation, hospitalization, or surgery) — adjudicated by a blinded event committee. Report the label definition explicitly; do not let "relapse" be site-defined ad hoc.
**Rationale:** Label reliability is a performance ceiling. Endoscopic scoring has documented inter-reader variability (the entire premise of the PMID 42238140 review), so the severity head cannot exceed adjudicated-reader consistency. "Relapse" has no single biomarker; fecal calprotectin is used as an inflammation proxy (NCT06698601) but is imperfect and assay-dependent, and clinical/endoscopic relapse can diverge — hence a composite adjudicated endpoint. ECCO guidance (PMID 36528797) and clinical management literature support standardized outcome definitions in IBD.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM

## What This Validation Certifies — and What It Does Not
**CERTIFIES:** That, if the prospective multi-center study and severity sub-study pass their pre-specified discrimination, calibration, and reader-agreement thresholds, the model can — as an adjunct to a gastroenterologist — stratify 12-month clinically-significant-relapse risk and reproduce an adjudicated endoscopic-severity index in adults with established Crohn's disease in remission/mild activity, on the specific endoscopy systems, calprotectin assays, and documentation contexts tested, within the pre-specified subgroups.
**DOES NOT CERTIFY:**
- That acting on the model's risk score improves patient outcomes (that requires an interventional/impact trial, not this observational validation).
- Physiological or biological ground truth of "flare" beyond the adjudicated composite label used.
- Clinical-grade equivalence to, or replacement of, gastroenterologist judgment or standard endoscopic scoring.
- Any FDA clearance, De Novo grant, or CDS-exemption status — regulatory determination is separate.
- Generalization beyond the studied population (e.g., pediatric IBD, ulcerative colitis, moderate-to-severe active disease), untested endoscopy vendors/software versions, or untested calprotectin assay platforms.
- Validity of the calprotectin/CRP inputs where pre-analytic assay conditions differ from those validated.
